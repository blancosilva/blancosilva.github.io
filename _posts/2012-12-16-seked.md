If a pyramid is 250 *cubits* high and the side of its base 360 *cubits* long, what is its *seked*?

<p style="text-align:center;margin-left:auto;margin-right:auto;width:100%;border:2px solid black;"><img src="https://i0.wp.com/farm9.staticflickr.com/8353/8279201319_00f003b2c2_o_d.jpg" style="width:75%; margin-left:auto; margin-right:auto;" /></p>

Take half of 360; it makes 180.  Multiply 250 so as to get 180; it makes \( 1/2 1/5 1/50 \) of a cubit.  A cubit is 7 palms.  Multiply 7 by \( 1/2 1/5 1/50: \)

<div>
	\begin{equation} 
	\begin{array}{l|lll} 
	1 & 7 & \\ 
	1/2 & 3 & 1/2 &  \\ 
	1/5  & 1 & 1/3 & 1/15 \\ 
	1/50 & 1/10 & 1/25 
	\end{array} 
	\end{equation}
</div>

The seked is \( 5 \tfrac{1}{25} \) palms [that is, \( (3+1/2) + (1+1/3+1/15) + (1/10+1/25) \)].

<div style="text-align:right;">A'h-mose. *The Rhind Papyrus. 33 AD*</div>
